Weight in sets

Same weight across all sets (straight sets) — choose a weight you can hit the lower rep range with in every set.

Example: Bench press 4×5–7 at 100 kg — all 4 sets at 100 kg.

Pyramid sets have a flaw — early sets too light, last set too heavy. Only 1–2 effective sets instead of 4.

When to increase weight

  • 1Complete the upper rep range in all sets (e.g. 4×7 with range 5–7) → add 2.5 kg next session.
  • 2After increasing, drop back to lower range (e.g. 4×5) and build back up. This is double progression.
  • 3For isolations and finishers, add 1.25 kg or one rep — slower progression is normal.
  • 4Can't hit the lower range for 2 weeks in a row — reduce by 5–10% and rebuild.

Warm-up

Before the first compound of each session — 2–3 warm-up sets, never to failure:

Set 1: 40% × 10 · Set 2: 60% × 6 · Set 3: 80% × 3

Goal: warm up joints, prime the nervous system, enter working sets fully ready.

Deload — every 6–8 weeks

Deload week: 50–60% of normal weight · 2–3 sets instead of 4 · same rep range but with 2–3 reps in reserve · cardio and abs unchanged.

Supercompensation fully happens during deload. Personal records often follow.
Session length: Push and Pull ~70 min · Legs ~75 min · + 25 min cardio · total ~95–100 min. Use a timer for rest periods.

💪 Muscle Gain (Lean Bulk)
Add +200–250 kcal above your TDEE. Aim for 0.5–1 kg gain per month — more than that means excess fat.
🔥 Fat Loss (Cut)
Subtract 300–400 kcal from your TDEE. Keep protein high to protect muscle. Aim for 0.5–0.75 kg loss per week.
Key rules:
· Protein: always 2.0–2.5 g per kg of bodyweight — this is non-negotiable
· Carbs: your main energy source, adjust based on training intensity
· Fat: minimum 0.8 g/kg for hormonal health, don't go below
· Weigh yourself weekly (same day, same time) — 4-week average matters more than daily fluctuation

📊 Body Measurements
How to measure correctly:
· Waist — at navel level, exhale naturally, don't suck in
· Chest — across nipple line, arms relaxed at sides
· Bicep — flexed, at widest point of dominant arm
· Shoulders — fullest part, arms at sides, tape parallel to floor
· Hips/Glutes — widest part of hips/buttocks
· Thigh — upper thigh, just below the glute fold, dominant leg
Measure in the morning, before eating, same conditions each time.
